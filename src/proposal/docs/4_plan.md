## 4.3 Team

The PI (materials engineering, additive manufacturing) and
Co-Investigator (vacuum-compatible drive train design) each
contribute 20% effort across the 12 months. A graduate student
(40% effort) executes the bench experiments under PI supervision.
A consulting expert in inline thermal-imaging QC supports Aim 3 at
40 hours over the full period.

## 4.4 Facilities

All work runs in the Example Institute of Selenology vacuum-chamber
facility (10<sup>-9</sup> Torr base pressure, 1 m × 1 m working
volume), with metallography and mechanical-test infrastructure
co-located. No subcontracted facilities are required.
