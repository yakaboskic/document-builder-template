# 5.0 Broader Impact <!-- \label{sec:impact} -->

## 5.1 Mission Relevance

A Phase II-qualified ISRU printer reduces the launch mass of the
first permanent lunar habitat by ≥40% by replacing structural cargo
with regolith-printed elements. The same technology generalizes
directly to Mars surface operations using basaltic regolith
analogues — the printer hardware is identical; only the sinter
profile changes.

## 5.2 Commercialization Pathway

Beyond the immediate NASA mission application, the Phase II
prototype provides the technology base for two commercial
trajectories:

- **Terrestrial vacuum AM**: industrial customers in semiconductor
  packaging and aerospace turbine components increasingly demand
  vacuum-compatible printers; our gantry IP transfers directly.
- **Lunar economy services**: as launch cadence increases, on-surface
  construction-as-a-service becomes a real market. The printer is
  the enabling capability.

## 5.3 Knowledge Transfer

All sintering profiles, gantry CAD, and QC training data developed
under Phase II are released as a permissively-licensed reference
design at the program's conclusion, with the explicit goal of
seeding a domestic ISRU supply chain.
