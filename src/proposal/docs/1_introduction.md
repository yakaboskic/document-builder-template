# 1.0 Introduction <!-- \label{sec:intro} -->

The cost of launching mass to the lunar surface is the dominant
constraint on every long-duration mission profile. Conventional
habitat designs treat structural materials as cargo and budget every
kilogram against transit fuel; under that model, surface infrastructure
scales linearly with launch tonnage and a single permanent outpost
remains an order of magnitude beyond program affordability.

This proposal generalizes our Phase I prototype into an operational
**in-situ resource utilization** (ISRU) platform that prints
load-bearing structures directly from lunar regolith. The Phase I
effort (Aug 2025–Feb 2026, \${{PHASE_I_BUDGET:,}}) sintered JSC-1A
regolith simulant into compression-test coupons reaching
{{COMPRESSIVE_STRENGTH_MPA:.1f}} MPa — comparable to a low-grade
masonry block — at a print resolution of {{PRINT_RESOLUTION_UM}} µm
\cite{phase1_final_2026}. Phase I demonstrated that the sinter
profile, gantry kinematics, and dust-tolerant powder bed all work as
an integrated system on Earth.

Phase II (12 months, \${{PHASE_II_BUDGET:,}} total) carries the
prototype from terrestrial vacuum-chamber operation to a
flight-qualifiable subsystem rated for the lunar thermal and
radiation environment. See Section 4.0 for the full milestone
schedule.
