# 2.0 Specific Aims <!-- \label{sec:aims} -->

## 2.1 Aim 1 — Vacuum-Compatible Sinter Profile

Develop and characterize a sinter profile that produces structurally
sound parts under lunar surface conditions (10<sup>-9</sup> Torr,
diurnal cycling between -173 °C and 117 °C). Phase I validated the
profile at 1 atm in a glovebox; Phase II re-runs the parameter sweep
in a thermal-vacuum chamber and quantifies the gap.

**Hypothesis**: a stepped 1380 °C → 1420 °C profile compensates for
reduced convective heat loss in vacuum and yields ≥25 MPa compressive
strength on JSC-1A coupons.

## 2.2 Aim 2 — Dust-Mitigation Recoater

Replace the Phase I roller recoater with a dust-tolerant blade
mechanism qualified for the abrasive fines characteristic of mature
lunar regolith. Failure mode analysis of Phase I prototypes showed
recoater binding as the leading defect source; this aim eliminates
that failure mode.

## 2.3 Aim 3 — Closed-Loop Quality Control

Integrate inline thermal-imaging-based defect detection into the
print path, with a per-layer go/no-go decision that pauses printing
on anomaly. Targets ≥95% defect-detection sensitivity at <2% false
positive rate, validated against ground-truth metallography.
